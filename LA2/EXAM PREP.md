
For simple isomorphisms, there is no need to prove them. For example, $$T(a_0+a_1x+...+a_nx^n)=\begin{bmatrix}a_0 \\ a_1 \\ ... \\a_n\end{bmatrix}$$
Remember exact definition of linear dependence lemma.
First condition is, we have an LD list $(v_1,...,v_n)$ and the first vector is not zero.
Then, there exists such $j \in \{1..n\}$  s.t. $v_j \in span(v_1,...,v_{j-1})$.
By definition of span, that is, there exist scalars s.t. $v_j = c_1v_1 + ... + c_{j-1}v_{j-1}$
