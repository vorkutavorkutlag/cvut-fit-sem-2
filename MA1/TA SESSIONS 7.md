
Function analysis with derivatives.

The derivative tells us how quickly the function is changing.
Negative - going down, Positive - going up (read left to right).
When it's zero, we have '0 speed'.

The derivative of $e^x$ at every point is $e^x$. Notice, that at $x=0,$ the derivative is $e^0=1$.
That is, the slope is $1$, meaning that the tangent is $y=x=1$.

Interestingly, we can see the same with the behavior of the sine function and its derivative, cosine. In the beginning, it is tangent to $x$. When cosine is zero, sine has an extreme.

The second derivative tells us about the behavior of the derivative. Say, place-time -> speed-time -> acceleration-time.
The second derivative tells us about the concavity.

Third derivative is jerk.

The derivative at zero is either a minimum, a maximum, or a saddle point.

Inflection point is when the second derivative changes sign.

First derivative changing sign means that we have a minima/maxima, not an inflection
