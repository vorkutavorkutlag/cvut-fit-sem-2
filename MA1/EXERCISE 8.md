`4.16)` for which real values $a \in R$ can the following function be extended to continuity at $x=0$? $f(x)=\frac{\ln(1+ax^2)}{x}$

If $a$ is zero, then the limit is zero.
Otherwise, multiply denominator and nominator by $ax$, we use the above identity, and get $1\cdot a\cdot 0 = 0$.
Therefore, the solution is, for all values of $a \in R$.
The continuous extension is $f(x)$ when $x \neq 0$, and $0$ when $x=0$.
That is extension of a function. $\forall a \in R$.
